Present Perfect Tense

The present perfect tense refers to an action or state that either occurred at an indefinite time in the past (e.g., we have talked before) or began in the past and continued to the present time (e.g., he has grown impatient over the last hour). This tense is formed by have/has + the past participle.

The present perfect of any verb is composed of two elements : the verb to have(present tense), plus the past participle of the main verb.The past participle of a regular verb is base+ed, e.g played, arrived, looked.

Example:

Slow Slowly
Quick Quickly
Amazing Amazingly
Clear Clearly

To change adjectives ending in 'y' into adverbs, change the 'y' to 'i' and add 'ly'.

Example

Happy Happily
Easy Easily
Busy Busily
Angry Angrily
